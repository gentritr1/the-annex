# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Players who enjoy narrative immersive simulations, philosophical dilemmas, stealth, and replayable consequence systems. The first release also serves internal playtesters and potential collaborators evaluating whether one compact case can make method, memory, and moral interpretation feel equally consequential.

## Product Purpose

The Annex is a deterministic browser-game vertical slice about auditing a contested identity inside a city whose civic infrastructure stores human memory. In one 15–25 minute case, the player investigates contradictory evidence through procedural, social, covert, or systems-led methods, then decides what legal personhood means under uncertainty. Success means the player understands the case without a tutorial wall, sees characters remember how they acted, reaches a genuinely difficult decision, and wants to replay to test another method.

## Brand Personality

Watchful, mechanical, and humane. The experience should feel like a municipal control room after midnight: precise enough to trust, rain-dark and quietly uncanny, but always centered on people rather than technology for its own sake.

## Anti-references

Generic neon-cyberpunk spectacle; purple-magenta synthwave; combat-first power fantasy; fake terminal noise that obscures the task; generic dashboard card grids; decorative glassmorphism; philosophical lore dumps; good-versus-evil morality meters; recognizable visual, narrative, or terminology homages to existing game franchises; unrestricted live-AI dialogue presented as authored truth.

## Design Principles

- Make the theme playable: every major interaction must test continuity, testimony, care, authority, or omission through an action.
- Remember the method, not only the outcome: characters and systems respond to how the player reached a decision.
- Keep truth deterministic: authored rules decide and persist; any future model may interpret or propose but never silently mutate canonical state.
- Prefer one rich case over shallow branch volume: routes diverge in evidence, trust, and meaning, then reconverge at a legible decision.
- Let atmosphere support comprehension: worldbuilding, motion, and sound cues must clarify state rather than compete with it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 Level AA. The full case must work with keyboard, pointer, and touch; focus must remain visible; text contrast must be robust; and no interaction may depend on hover, color, audio, or timing alone. Reduced motion, high-contrast evidence states, readable text sizing, subtitles-ready dialogue structure, and a no-time-pressure default are foundational requirements. Content should treat identity, memory loss, care, and institutional harm with specificity rather than spectacle.
